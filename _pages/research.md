## WORK IN PROGRESS

### "Does the Financial Accelerator accelerate inequalities?" (draft coming soon)

This study examines the redistribution effects of a conventional monetary pol- icy shock among households in the presence of financial frictions on the production side of the economy. In this regard, I adopt a Heterogeneous Agents New Key- nesian model that features a financial accelerator. The results show that with contractionary monetary policy, the Gini coefficient of wealth and other inequal- ity measures are significantly higher in relative terms than in the counterfactual scenario with no frictions, proving that wealth inequality is substantially ”acceler- ated” by firms’ financial characteristics. Furthermore, consumption dynamics are also affected, suggesting that financial frictions have considerable effects on how households consume and save along their distribution, since they rely differently on labor income for consumption smoothing and have different marginal propensities to consume.
